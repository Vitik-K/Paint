Watching Paint Dry

Is it as boring as it sounds?

Paint Composition

Paints usually contain three key components: pigments, binders and additives. Pigments are dry, insoluble powders that by wave length-selective absorption (ie they only reflect certain wavelengths) change the colour of reflected or transmitted light, giving paint its colorization. They can be ascertained either naturally or produced synthetically.

Mechanism of Paint Drying

The majority of paints dry through evaporation, a process that allows its pigmentation to be set onto a chosen surface purely by being exposed to the surrounding atmosphere. However, the drying process can vary and is often complicated and altered between different paint types.

2012 Watching Paint Dry Championship

In 2012, the watching paint dry championship was held in entral England. The top 64 entrants from around the world participated in a preliminary round where they stared at a wall freshly painted with their favorite color. Winners from this round advanced to the semi-final and final rounds where they had to stare at a wall freshly painted with the color they most disliked. The winner walked away with prize of $100,000 and a new coat of paint for their home.

An expression origin story.

"Watching paint dry", closely related to "watching the grass grow", is an English-language idiom that means something is incredibly boring or tedious. It is thought to have originated in the United States. A well known (maybe?) example of this phrase being used in media is from 1969, where sports announcer Red Baber stated that watching baseball was " as exciting as watching paint dry".

In 2016, British film maker Charlie Lyne created a 10 hour long film titled 'Paint Drying'. This amazing cinamatic masterpiece was all about, you guessed it, paint drying and was made specifically to protest against the British Board of Film Classification and their high fees and censorship.
